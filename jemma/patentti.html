Method for three-dimensional viewing.

This method constitutes of a computer capable of recognizing pair of human eyes in a crowd, and picture-projecting device which can project different view to each of those eyes. This allows life-like representation of three-dimensional object, as computer is aware of distance and position of a human and can change the pictures projected accordingly.

The picture-projecting device

The device consists of three parts:

  1.   Transparent colored picture screen, capable of showing different pictures hundreds or thousands times per second. Best-known current technology would be a large Liquid Crystal plate.
  2.   Light emitting screen that can show black and white images hundreds or thousands times per second. This screen should have very high light intensity. Current known techologies are plasma plates, led-array plates and a lamp behind a Liquid Crystal plate.
  3.   Large lens behind the screen number one. This lens projects light from emitting dot from the screen number two to the viewer's eye. The eye will see the screen number one lighted evenly from this one dot. In a crowd several ligth-emitting dots will allow same scene to be viewed by several eyes at the same time. Unlike in the picture below the lens would be flat, ie a Fresnell lens, made of array of prims.

 Details of operation.

The computer recognizes human eyes from certain distance and projectes individual pictrures into each of them at least 30 times per second. The screen is not blank for those eyes that are not recognized, as the projector can be used to send special message to everybody else. I case of public display this might be just a text "Please move closer".

Those within suitable distance can experience real 3D-object. As the viewer moves sideways, he/she will get different view suitable for that viewing angle.

Projectors can be arranged to a cube and every angle will give different view of the object within the cube.

Or projectors can arranged onto walls of a room and viewers will see real-looking objects within the room.
